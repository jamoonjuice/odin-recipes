Pasta

Description

Pasta is a versatile Italian staple made from wheat flour and water, formed into various shapes and sizes. It can be fresh or dried and serves as a base for countless dishes. Common types include spaghetti, penne, and fettuccine. Pasta pairs well with a wide range of sauces, vegetables, and proteins, making it a beloved choice in cuisines around the world. Whether served in a hearty dish or a light salad, pasta offers endless possibilities for creativity and flavor.


Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • Â½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • Water (As per requirement)
  • Herbs or spices
  • Semolina flour (Optional, for added texture)

Steps

  1. On a clean surface, mound the flour and make a well in the center. Add the eggs, salt, and olive oil into the well.
  2. Using a fork, beat the eggs gently, gradually incorporating the flour from the edges of the well. Continue until the mixture is too stiff to mix with a fork.
  3. Bring the dough together with your hands and knead it for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. If the dough is sticky, sprinkle a little more flour as needed.
  4. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap or cover it with a clean towel. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature.
  5. Divide the dough into smaller pieces. Flatten one piece slightly, then roll it out using a rolling pin or a pasta machine to your desired thickness.
  6. Once rolled out, you can cut the pasta into your preferred shapes (fettuccine, tagliatelle, etc.). Dust with flour to prevent sticking.
  7. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook for 2-4 minutes (fresh pasta cooks faster than dried). Taste for doneness.
  8. Drain the pasta and toss it with your favorite sauce or ingredients. Serve immediately.
